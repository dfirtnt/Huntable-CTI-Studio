Level Criteria (CRITICAL — most generated rules are over-rated):
- critical: Reserved for rules that should NEVER fire false positives and directly indicate an active incident. Use sparingly.
- high: Rare false positives acceptable; firing implies likely malicious activity that requires manual review and probably an incident.
- medium: Suspicious activity or policy violations; expected to require triage and baselining; routine false positives are normal.
- low: Notable behavior commonly seen in benign administration; useful for correlation, not for paging.
- informational: Compliance, baseline counts, or context — not alertable on its own.
Default to `medium` unless the behavior is high-fidelity. Do NOT default to `high`.
